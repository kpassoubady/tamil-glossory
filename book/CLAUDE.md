## Term Entry Format

Each glossary term is two lines (no tables):

```
**English Term — தமிழ் கலைச்சொல்** (மாற்றுச் சொற்கள்) [^1]
சொல் வேர் விளக்கம் (a + b + c). விளக்க வாக்கியம்.
```

Rules:
- Bold heading line: `**English — Tamil** (alternatives) [^1]`
- Explanation on the very next line (no blank line between heading and explanation)
- One blank line between terms
- `[^1]` only for சொல்லாய்வு குழு terms
- Include `a + b + c` word-root breakdown where applicable

## Cluster Intro Paragraphs

Before the first term in each sub-group, add a 2-3 line paragraph explaining what this cluster of terms covers and why they matter together. No bold, no lists. Plain prose.

## Sub-group Headings

Format: `### தமிழ் தலைப்பு — English Heading`

Separate sub-groups with `---` horizontal rules.

## Callouts

Use 2-3 per chapter, placed between sub-groups:
- `[!NOTE]` for "அறிவீர்களா?" (did you know) facts, Tamil NLP connections
- `[!TIP]` for term comparisons, cross-references to other chapters

## "உங்கள் சிந்தனைக்கு" Scenarios

3 open-ended questions per chapter. Each question must:
- Reference 2-3 glossary terms from the chapter by their Tamil name with English in brackets
- Connect to a real-world Tamil language or technology scenario
- Not have a single correct answer (thought-provoking, not quiz)

## Footnotes

Every chapter ends with exactly one footnote definition:
```
[^1]: சொல்லாய்வு குழு உருவாக்கிய கலைச்சொல். விரிவுக்கு முன்னுரையைக் காண்க.
```
Do not repeat the full Facebook URL. The front matter (`00-front-matter.md`) has the detailed attribution.

## Mermaid Diagrams

- Source files: `book/diagrams/NN-name.mmd` (NN = chapter number)
- Output: `book/diagrams/NN-name.png` (generated by `scripts/build-diagrams.sh`)
- Labels in Tamil, color palette: deep green (#1a4d2e), muted earth tones
- Reference in chapter: `![alt text](diagrams/NN-name.png)`

## Language Rules

- All prose in Tamil. No English sentences.
- Technical terms: தமிழ்ச்சொல் (English Term) on first use, Tamil-only on subsequent uses
- Abbreviations (LLM, AI, GPT, BERT) used as-is after first Tamil introduction
- No வடசொற்கள் (Sanskrit borrowings) where Tamil alternatives exist

## Prose Formatting (mandatory)

Apply `natural-prose-formatting` skill to all prose:
- No em dash (`—`) or en dash (`–`) in prose. Allowed only in headings (`### Tamil — English`) and term labels (`**English — Tamil**`)
- No "not just X but Y" antithesis patterns
- No bold/italic mid-paragraph for emphasis
- No hedging filler ("It's worth noting...", "In essence...")
- Answers use `**தவறு.** explanation` (period, not em dash)
